How to Use the Mining Material Finder

Start on the Locations & Percentages tab and choose a system — Stanton, Pyro, or Nyx — using the tabs above the location grid. Select up to three materials you need from the dropdown filters; the tool instantly narrows the list to locations that contain all of them and sorts the results by combined yield, so the best spot lands at the top. Each location card breaks results down by mining method (Ship, ROC, or Hand) so you know exactly what equipment to bring before you fly out. Prefer scanning in the field instead? Switch to the Signature Checker tab and enter the raw RS reading from your ship's HUD — the tool runs the cluster math instantly and tells you the material, rarity tier, and node count before you commit to a burn. The underlying data is rebuilt from the game's files for every major mining patch, including 4.8.3.

How to Read Ship, ROC, and Hand Mining Percentages

Every location card lists three potential mining methods — Ship, ROC, and Hand — and each material under those headings shows a percentage. That number is not how much of the material exists at the location; it's the statistical likelihood of encountering that specific resource when you scan or break open a node using that method at that location. A material listed at 35% under Ship Mining means roughly one in three ship-mineable deposits you crack open there will yield that resource, while the remaining nodes are made up of the other materials listed in that same group. Because the three methods pull from separate node pools, a location can have a high Quantainium chance via Ship mining and a completely different profile via Hand or ROC mining. Always check the method that matches the equipment you're flying with — a location with great Ship percentages won't help you if you're running a ROC on the surface.

Best Locations for Hadanite, Quantainium, and Rare Ore in 4.8.3

If you're optimizing a mining loop for Star Citizen 4.8.3, Hadanite belongs at the top of the list. This pink-purple mineral is one of the strongest targets for ROC mining loops right now, and its richest known concentration sits on Aberdeen, the moon orbiting Crusader in Stanton, where dense surface clusters let a ROC pilot chain nodes with minimal travel time. Quantainium remains the headline Ship and ROC target across both systems, though it decays fast after extraction, so route planning matters as much as the percentage — filter for it above to find a high-yield deposit close to a refinery. Rarer ores like Taranite, Bexalite, and Riccite tend to cluster in Pyro's less-traveled fields, rewarding pilots willing to fly further for a heavier payout per scoop. Use the material filters above rather than a static guide, since exact percentages and hot spots shift between patches.

System Breakdown: Stanton vs. Pyro

Stanton is the original, UEE-regulated system, and it shows in the mineral data: decades of corporate and player mining activity have thinned out the highest-purity nodes, so the average resource quality you'll encounter there skews lower, even though it's by far the safer system to operate in. Pyro is newer, lawless, and largely unmined, which means its asteroid fields and surface deposits still carry a noticeably higher concentration of high-tier materials. That's the basis of the Pro Tip above: if raw yield and material quality are your priority, Pyro consistently outperforms Stanton, at the cost of UEE security and longer, riskier supply runs. A handful of materials, including Sadaryx and Savrilium, are currently exclusive to Rock Breaker Contract locations regardless of system. Mining is also one of the most repeatable ways to grind aUEC, whether you're saving toward a bigger hull or just covering routine repairs and ammo — chaining high-percentage Ship and ROC locations through the filters above gets you to a usable total faster than mining wherever you happen to be.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I use the RS signature scanner?

Switch to the Signature Checker tab and type the raw RS number shown on your ship's mining HUD into the scanner input. The tool runs exact cluster math against the full signature database and instantly returns the matching material, rarity tier, mining method, and how many nodes make up that cluster.

Where is the best place to mine Hadanite in Star Citizen?

As of Patch 4.8, Aberdeen — the moon orbiting Crusader in Stanton — has the richest known concentration of Hadanite, with dense surface rock clusters packed close together that make it one of the strongest ROC mining loops in the game. Use the system and material filters above to confirm the current highest-percentage Hadanite nodes, since drop tables can shift between patches.

Where do I find Quantainium in Patch 4.8.3?

Quantainium can be found via Ship and ROC mining in select asteroid clusters and surface deposits across both Stanton and Pyro — filter for it above to find the best current node before it decays.
